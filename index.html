Plain English

A digital dollar.
Nothing more.

Stablecoins get talked about in the same breath as crypto. They shouldn't be. Here's what they actually are, and why they belong in a corporate payments stack.

A stablecoin is a digital representation of U.S. currency, tied 1:1 to the value of one dollar — backed by real dollar-denominated assets and engineered to avoid the price swings that define volatile cryptocurrencies.

For Corpay customers, stablecoin represents fast, certain settlement, 24/7 availability, and a global payment rail that complements — rather than replaces — traditional wires, ACH, and card rails.

Most business stablecoin activity today centers around USDC, a fully backed digital dollar widely adopted by financial services platforms and institutional users worldwide.

Regulatory framework

The first federal
law for payment stablecoins.

In July 2025, the U.S. enacted the GENIUS Act — the first federal framework for payment stablecoins. It puts USDC and similar instruments on a clear legal path for commercial use.

Public Law
The GENIUS
Act
Federal Stablecoin Framework
Enacted July 2025
  1. 01 Requires stablecoins to be fully backed by high-quality liquid assets such as U.S. dollars or short-term U.S. Treasury instruments.
  2. 02 Establishes consumer protections and transparency requirements for issuers and users.
  3. 03 Provides regulatory clarity for stablecoin issuers, treasury teams, and compliance officers.
  4. 04 Reinforces the role of the U.S. dollar in digital payment systems globally.
